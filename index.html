Give Protocol Foundation

Building open-source infrastructure for transparent global philanthropy.

The global charitable sector moves $500B+ annually. A significant share is lost to transfer fees, currency conversion, and access gaps that exclude the organizations doing the hardest work from international funding flows entirely. We build the tools to fix that.

See the portfolio Board & partnerships

These friction costs — 4–12% lost to transfer fees, 3–10 days for funds to arrive, organizations without traditional banking excluded from international flows entirely — don't just reduce efficiency. They shape which causes receive funding and which organizations can participate in global philanthropy at all. We build open-source infrastructure to change that, for the entire sector, not just our balance sheet.

How we choose what to build

A short list of things we won't compromise.

We pursue a new product only when every one of these holds. If any is missing, we wait — sunk effort is never a reason to keep going. Measured growth with compliance-first priorities: we'd rather move carefully and build trust than scale prematurely.

Public good first

A real infrastructure gap in a market that's underserved or genuinely disruptable — not a marginal improvement to something already well served.

Open by default

Buildable on open-source, community-first principles. If it only works through closed code or extractive monetization, it doesn't belong here.

Domain-led

A volunteer leader with genuine expertise and energy to carry it through the gap between concept and traction. Not founder hubris.

Sustainable, not extractive

A credible path to covering its own costs — visible even if not immediate — that never asks the mission to bend toward revenue.
